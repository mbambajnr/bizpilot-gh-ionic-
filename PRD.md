BizPilot Product Requirements Document (Final Product)  
1\. Product Name  
**BizPilot**  
2\. Product Vision  
BizPilot is a mobile-first business operating system for small and growing businesses in Africa, starting with Ghana, designed to help merchants, retailers, distributors, and service-led SMEs manage sales, inventory, customer balances, quotations, payments, receipts, and operational visibility from one simple platform.  
BizPilot is built to give small businesses the structure of an ERP without the complexity of enterprise software.  
3\. Product Mission  
Enable African small businesses to run daily operations with confidence using a simple, fast, mobile-first system that tracks stock, sales, receivables, payments, and business performance in real time.  
4\. Problem Statement  
Small businesses across Ghana and similar markets often run operations using:  
• paper records  
• WhatsApp chats  
• notebooks  
• spreadsheets  
• memory  
• disconnected tools  
This leads to:  
• inventory errors  
• lost sales visibility  
• weak receivables tracking  
• poor customer follow-up  
• no audit trail  
• cash leakage  
• weak reporting  
• difficulty scaling operations  
Existing ERP tools are often too expensive, too complex, desktop-heavy, or poorly adapted for the workflow realities of African SMEs.  
BizPilot solves this by providing an operational system that is:  
• mobile-first  
• easy to use  
• structured like a business system  
• lightweight enough for small businesses  
• extensible enough to grow over time  
5\. Product Goals  
Primary goals  
• simplify daily business operations for SMEs

10\. System Architecture Requirements  
10.1 Data Model Principles  
BizPilot must separate:  
• **master data**  
• **transactions**  
• **derived views**  
Master data  
• business profile  
• products  
• customers  
• suppliers  
• users  
• branches  
Transactions  
• sales  
• quotations  
• payments  
• stock movements  
• expense entries  
• purchase entries  
• reversals/corrections  
Derived views  
• dashboard metrics  
• reports  
• low-stock summaries  
• revenue trends  
• customer balance summaries  
10.2 Transaction-first Architecture  
All critical balances should be explainable through underlying transaction history.  
This includes:  
• stock quantity  
• customer balance  
• receivables  
• cash totals  
• payment histories  
10.3 Status-driven Workflows  
Key entities must use explicit statuses rather than ambiguous implicit state.  
10.4 Document Relationships  
The system must link:  
• quotation → sale  
• sale → receipt/invoice  
• sale → payment entries  
• sale → stock movement  
• reversal/correction → original document  
• purchase → stock receipt
